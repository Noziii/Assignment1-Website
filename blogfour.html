My website has gone through a tremendous amount of changes this quarter. Most of which were last-minute changes, I had to change a lot of the aesthetic of my page at the start of the quarter my website looked more like a website for makeup or photography. Which is not what I was aiming for I then had to change it to fit the message I am trying to put across to whoever views my website. And that is trying to sell me as a web developer, my target audience needs to understand the type of person I am by just viewing my website and the way in which elements on my site are organized or designed. But also making sure that my website is easy to navigate, and that text is easy to read. Important text is written in bold enough letters to make them eligible to, viewers. I struggled a bit with developing good UI and UX although I got a few of my friends at home to navigate through my website without being told what to press and where to press. The site itself is easy enough to understand. But this could be based on their previous experience with most websites as the setup is no different. What I struggled with a lot was my portfolio page. Where I implemented a picture overlay but for the second picture the overlay is rendering outside the image and that does cause a malfunction in the site. For the most part, the process was rather stressful and one that I had underestimated because a large deal of consideration and thought goes into a website. At first glance, a website might look simply but it is a very taxing process, where you can spend hours trying to fix the position, width, and height of an image.
